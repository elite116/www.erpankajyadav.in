The 2.25 MFD capacitor is usually not available in the local market and they instead fool the people that instead of it use 2.5 MFD so that the speed will increase and you will get better performance. The Electricity Bill(Article)

August 20, 2020

Many times you might have seen that in your Bill ,that there is one thing called power factor and MDI . Actually they are the points of your load demand for your home.
But , the loop hole lies here are as follows:-
1. Many distribution companies simply keeps power factor as “1″ in many domestic consumer bill which is impossible in this world because it means the home as not even a single appliance which produces heat .and how company then gets the profit as because the formula for active power calculation is V*I*cos(phi) which we pays. So , when this cos(phi) is 1 that means for V=230 and I=10, the unit consumed is “2.3 units”. Where as if phi is 0.8 which is in actual for homes then the units consumed is “1.8 units “. So for a state of 1.5 crore consumers this small factor each month amounts to tens of crores of rupees without getting consumed by the consumer. This is a cheat as you can ask your meter reader for the power factor reading of the month.
2. The MDI is Max. Demand Indicator . which is as per the gov.t of India’s notified norms is used to detect the max. current demand of the house for a particular month .So ,when you run say a washing machine and an AC simultaneously ,then the current demand obviously increases and the company records it and for a fixed no. of times in a year it will be checked and then they are liable to increase your load demand .So say if in a house, a marriage or a renovation work is happening, then this MDI gets obviously recorded ,but logically it's not the true load of the home ( as they don't organize the marriage for a full year) . This is where the loop hole resides. The company simply increases it and you will pay more. You can write an application for it that the load was for a particular concern and not the actual load as whenever the sanction load demand increases, then the per unit slab also increases. So you can save a lot as a bill of ₹1000 for particular units of consumption can go for ₹6000(along with fixed charges increases).
